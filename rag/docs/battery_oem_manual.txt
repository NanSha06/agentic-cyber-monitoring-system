BATTERY OEM SAFETY MANUAL — LITHIUM-ION PRISMATIC CELLS
Version 3.2 | Classification: Internal Use

1. THERMAL MANAGEMENT
Lithium-ion cells operate safely between -20°C and 60°C. Sustained operation above 45°C accelerates electrolyte decomposition and SEI layer growth, reducing State of Health (SOH) by approximately 2-4% per 100 cycles at elevated temperatures.

Thermal Runaway Precursors:
- Temperature rate of change exceeding 2°C/min without load increase
- Voltage drop below 3.0V under normal load conditions
- Swelling or case deformation (physical inspection required)
- Electrolyte venting (detectable as increase in ambient humidity near cell)

Thermal Runaway Cascade Sequence:
Stage 1 (70-90°C): Electrolyte decomposition begins, gas generation starts
Stage 2 (90-120°C): SEI layer breakdown, lithium metal deposition
Stage 3 (120-150°C): Separator melting, internal short circuit
Stage 4 (>150°C): Thermal runaway propagation, fire or explosion risk

2. STATE OF HEALTH (SOH) THRESHOLDS
- SOH > 80%: Normal operation, standard monitoring
- SOH 60-80%: Degraded, increase monitoring frequency to 5-minute intervals
- SOH 40-60%: Critical degradation, schedule replacement within 30 days
- SOH < 40%: Immediate decommission, do not operate under load

3. STATE OF CHARGE (SOC) MANAGEMENT
Optimal long-term SOC range: 20-80%
Deep discharge (<10% SOC) causes copper dissolution from anode current collector.
Sustained overcharge (>95% SOC) accelerates lithium plating.

4. REMAINING USEFUL LIFE (RUL) ESTIMATION
RUL is calculated from cycle count, depth of discharge history, and temperature exposure.
Battery approaching end-of-life shows:
- Increased internal resistance (voltage sag under load >150mV)
- Reduced capacity retention (<80% of nominal)
- Elevated self-discharge rate (>3% per day at room temperature)

5. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES
If thermal runaway detected:
1. Immediately disconnect load and charging source
2. Do NOT use water — use Class D extinguisher or dry sand
3. Evacuate area within 10 meters
4. Contact safety team: thermal events can reignite hours after initial suppression
5. Document: asset_id, timestamp, last SOH reading, ambient temperature
